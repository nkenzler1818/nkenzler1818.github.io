Capstone Project

A dynamic business simulation showcasing market strategy, competition, and adaptive decision-making over time.

Project Overview

In this capstone, we participated in a weekly business simulation called BSG, where our team ran a virtual shoe company. Each week, we received a report on Sunday night that reflected how our decisions stacked up against other teams in the market. We then had a few days to adjust our strategy before the next round.

At the start, we chose a niche market position—deciding whether to focus on high-quality, premium shoes or lower-cost, high-volume production. I was the designated decision coordinator, responsible for consolidating team inputs and submitting a unified strategy each week to avoid conflicting changes.

Key Components & What Happened

Weekly Competitive Adjustments

Each week, we analyzed Sunday reports to assess performance against competing teams. Several rivals built an early lead, creating pressure to adapt.

Finding Our Niche

Many teams aggressively pursued internet sales, making it highly competitive. I identified wholesale production as an underutilized channel and shifted our focus there, allowing us to gain market share with less direct competition while maintaining selective internet sales.

Climbing the Ranks

Despite early setbacks, we steadily improved performance. By the end of the simulation:

  • • Stock price reached $559
  • • Earnings per share rose to $23
  • • Return on equity peaked at 43%

Highlighting Our Success

We finished with:

  • • A+ credit rating
  • • A+ image rating
  • • $1.8M in total sales

This contrasted sharply with teams whose stock prices fell as low as $21.

Conclusion: The Turnaround and Final Outcomes

In the images (to be labeled later), the results of our strategic shift are highlighted. In Year 13, a strategic mistake caused a significant ranking drop. By Year 15, our recovery was clear — improved S/Q ratings, refined strategy, and recovered sales allowed us to climb back up the rankings and finish strong.

This project demonstrates how strategic pivots, data-driven decision-making, and strong team coordination can drive success in competitive environments.
